Privacy & Data Security

Everything stays on your device

All round data, player names, handicaps, betting results, and Golf Canada member IDs are stored exclusively in your iPhone’s browser storage (localStorage). This data never leaves your device and is never sent to any server, cloud service, or third party. There is no Nassau Golf database or backend — the app is a single file that runs entirely on your phone.

Golf Canada Member ID & Password

When you enter your Golf Canada credentials, they are used for one purpose only: fetching your current handicap index directly from Golf Canada’s API. Your credentials are:

  • Stored only in your device’s local browser storage
  • Never transmitted to any server other than Golf Canada’s own secure API
  • Never shared with other players, the app developer, or any third party
  • Never included in round history exports or backups

Once saved, your handicap refreshes automatically every 3 hours in the background. Your credentials are read locally each time and sent directly to Golf Canada. No one else ever sees them.

How long credentials are remembered

Your Golf Canada credentials persist indefinitely and survive app updates, closing the app, and restarting your phone. They are only lost if you manually clear Safari website data, or set up a brand new iPhone without restoring from an iCloud backup.

Weather data

The app fetches live weather from the Derrick GWC weather station via a secure Cloudflare Worker proxy. No personal information is sent with this request — it is a read-only call to a weather instrument.

Need help with Golf Canada credentials?

If you don’t have a Golf Canada account or have forgotten your credentials, contact Trevor Goplin, Golf Professional at Derrick Golf & Winter Club. He can look up your member ID and assist with account setup at golfcanada.ca.

🔒 Nassau Golf does not collect analytics, does not serve ads, has no user accounts, and has no server. Your data is yours alone.

This button was previously called “Round, Match & Player Settings” and lived below the scorecard. It now sits directly beneath the score wheels so it’s easy to reach while scoring.

Toggle automatic hole progression on or off mid-round. When ON, the app moves to the next hole automatically once all players have a score entered.

Each player row shows their handicap index (display-only) and a Tee Deck selector. Select a different deck and the handicap recalculates automatically. All matches update instantly. Change the handicap by selecting a new Tee Deck — the field is not directly editable.

  • Add Player — if fewer than 5 players are in, a + Add Player button appears. The new player starts with no scores and is not added to existing matches automatically.

1. Installing Nassau Golf on Your iPhone

Nassau Golf is a Progressive Web App (PWA). It is not downloaded from the App Store — instead, you add it to your Home Screen directly from Safari. Once installed, it opens full-screen, works without an internet connection during your round, and keeps your round data saved on your device.

⚠️ You MUST install the app to your device before your data will be reliably saved. If you only open the web address in a browser tab, your saved players, favorites, and round history can be cleared by the browser at any time. Install it first, then always open it from the installed icon.
⚠️ Do NOT use the “Remove App” option on the Home Screen icon — on iOS this wipes the app’s local data including your saved players and round history. To update or reinstall, use the Safari method below instead.

Step-by-Step Installation

  • Open Safari on your iPhone (it must be Safari).
  • Go to https://bdcomeau.github.io/nassau-golf/
  • Tap the Share button (the square with an up arrow).
  • Scroll down and tap “Add to Home Screen.”
  • Tap “Add” in the top-right corner.
  • A Nassau Golf icon appears on your Home Screen.
Tip: Always launch the app from the Home Screen icon, not from a Safari bookmark. The Home Screen icon runs in standalone mode, which is required for the app to work correctly.

Keeping the App Current

Nassau Golf updates automatically in the background. You can also check on demand by tapping Update App in the action bar at the bottom of the Setup screen — it shows the current version right on the button.

  • Tap Update App — checks for a newer version and updates if found.
  • Force-close and reopen — swipe the app up in the app switcher, then reopen with Wi-Fi or signal so the latest version can download.
  • Clear Safari site data — Settings → Safari → Advanced → Website Data, find bdcomeau.github.io, swipe left and Delete, then reopen. This does NOT delete your data.
⚠️ Do NOT delete the Home Screen icon and reinstall. On iOS, removing a PWA icon permanently erases all its local data. Use Update App, force-close, or the Safari data method above.

Protecting Your Data

Your saved players, favorites, and round history live in local storage tied to the installed app. As an extra safeguard, back up regularly using the 💾 Backup button in the action bar (Section 15). A backup takes seconds.

2. Setting Up a Round

When you open Nassau Golf, you’ll land on the Setup screen — add players, set handicaps, choose your starting hole, and configure games. Common actions live in a fixed bar pinned to the bottom of the screen (Section 3).

Adding Players

  • Use + and − to set the number of players (1 to 5). A fresh setup starts with a single player — you (the “Me” favorite).
  • Enter each player’s name and current handicap index.
  • Optionally enter a 10-digit Golf Canada Member ID.
  • Each row has Fetch, Save, and (once filled) Clear pills on the right.
  • Use the “— saved players —” dropdown to load a saved player into any slot.
Tip: As you type each name, the match buttons below update live to show real names instead of “P1,” “P2.”
Tip: Each golfer must be unique. Duplicate names or IDs are outlined in red and the round won’t start until fixed.

Favorites — Your Regular Group

Save your regulars as Favorites and have them fill in automatically each round. The Setup screen has Load My Favorites and Manage Favorites buttons side by side near the top.

  • Save your regular players first (the Save pill, or a Golf Canada lookup).
  • Tap ⭐ Manage Favorites to open the favorites list.
  • Star up to five players you golf with regularly.
  • Mark one as “Me” — that’s you, the scorekeeper, always in slot 1.
  • Optionally set each player’s default tee deck.

On a fresh start, just your “Me” favorite loads into slot 1. Tap ⭐ Load My Favorites to pull in the rest of your group on demand. Auto-fill only happens when rows are empty, so it never overwrites names you’ve started typing. You can have at most five favorites.

Tip: Favorites — including each player’s default tee deck — are stored with your saved players, so they’re included in your backups (Section 15).

Looking Up a Handicap from Golf Canada

Each player row has a green Golf Canada Fetch pill. Enter the player’s name and 10-digit Member ID, then tap Fetch — the handicap fills in automatically and the player is saved to your contacts.

Tip: A successful lookup saves the player automatically. If they already exist, their handicap updates in place (no duplicate).
⚠️ Your Golf Canada password is stored on your device so you don’t re-enter it each round. It’s only ever sent to the lookup service. Prefer to type handicaps manually if you’d rather not store it.

Automatic Handicap Refresh

Once a player has a Member Number saved, the app keeps their handicap current — quietly refreshing in the background whenever players load. A handicap is only re-fetched if it’s more than about three hours old. Auto-refresh is silent and leaves the existing handicap in place if you’re offline.

Tee Decks & Handicap Adjustment

Each golfer selects a tee deck — DECK1, DECK1/2, DECK2, DECK2/3, DECK3, DECK3/4, or DECK4, listed longest to shortest. The app adjusts the fetched Golf Canada index for the chosen deck and rounds it to a whole number, which becomes that player’s playing handicap.

Tee DeckPlaying handicap (from the fetched index)
DECK1round( index × 121 ÷ 113 + 0.9 )
DECK1/2round( index × 120 ÷ 113 + 0.2 )
DECK2round( index × 119 ÷ 113 − 0.4 )
DECK2/3round( index × 118 ÷ 113 − 1.8 )
DECK3round( index × 117 ÷ 113 − 2.9 )

Each handicap shows as two boxes: IDX (the decimal index, e.g. 7.5) and ADJ (the rounded playing handicap for the deck). The ADJ number drives the matches. New players default to DECK2.

⚠️ A handicap you type by hand is used exactly as entered — no deck adjustment. Use the Golf Canada lookup for automatic per-deck adjustment.

Choosing a Starting Hole

For shotgun starts, use the Starting Hole selector (1–18). All scoring, match calculations, and hole badges adjust automatically.

Round Type — Scorecard Only

Switch on “Scorecard only” when you just want to keep score — no betting games. Every game section disappears from setup and the game tabs are hidden. The master scorecard shows no money rows. Everything else still works: scores, handicaps, stroke circles, timing, weather, Birdie Juice, and history.

Tip: Scorecard-only is per round. The setting resets to off when you start a new round.

“What If You’d Played Nassau?”

In a scorecard-only round, once all 18 holes are scored, a “🤔 What if you’d played Nassau?” banner appears. Tap it to see what each matchup would have paid, using the full Nassau engine. A “$ per side” selector defaults to $2. Nothing is saved — it’s purely for fun.

Choosing Your Games

Set up any combination of games. The games offered depend on how many players are in the round:

PlayersGames available
1 playerMatch Cards only.
2 players1v1 Nassau, Snake, Stableford, Skins, and Match Cards.
3 playersThe above plus 6-Point.
4 playersEvery game, including 2v2 Nassau and Vegas.
5 playersEvery game. Vegas is played by any four of the five (pick two teams of two); the fifth sits out Vegas but plays everything else.

For a multi-player betting round, at least one game is required to start — or switch on Scorecard only. Each game has its own collapsible card in the Betting Games area (all start collapsed — tap a header to expand).

Other Setup Details

  • Golf Canada ID Lock — a loaded player with an ID on file shows the ID field locked (🔒). Tap to unlock if you must change it.
  • Clearing a Player Row — the red ✕ Clear pill wipes the name, GC ID, and handicap for that row.
  • Duplicate Prevention — the saved-players dropdown greys out anyone already in another row.
  • Pre-Loaded Players — the regular Derrick GWC group (24 players with IDs) is pre-loaded and merged in on every launch.
  • Solo Mode — with one player the round keeps score only, but Match Cards is available. Add a second player to unlock the betting games.

Starting the Round

Tap Start Round. The app jumps straight to the Scorecard screen, ready for the first hole.

6. The Nassau Tab

Tap the 💵 Games button, then Nassau, to see live match scorecards for every Nassau game.

Reading a Match Scorecard

  • Each match shows Hole, HCP, Par, each player’s gross scores, then running columns for Nassau F9, B9, 18, and each press.
  • Handicaps here are the ADJUSTED match handicaps — the lowest drops to scratch and the others play off that.
  • Stroke holes are pre-marked with red circles.
  • After the 17th hole, a 🌍 globe marks any bet eligible for The International (Section 7).

Presses

A press is an extra bet worth DOUBLE the Nassau amount, starting automatically the moment the parent game first reaches 2 up or 2 down.

  • P1 starts when the overall 18 game first hits ±2; P2 when P1 hits ±2; and so on.
  • A press started on the front nine settles in two halves (at hole 9, then hole 18), each worth the press amount.
  • A press started on the back nine is a single bet, settled at hole 18.
  • After 18 holes, any bet that isn’t even pays out.

Result Banner & WINS Row

Once all 18 holes are scored, a gold banner shows the final result — e.g. “Greg Hines wins $38” or “All square — $0.” The WINS row shows each bet’s settlement from the first side’s perspective; a bet folded into the International shows 🌍.

7. The International (“The Big I”)

The International — “The Big I” — is an optional side bet available only after the 17th hole of an 18-hole Nassau. The trailing side may ask for it; the leading side accepts or declines. Agreed verbally at the tee, then engaged in the app.

Leaders and Trailers

The side UP in total money after 17 holes are the Leaders; the other side are the Trailers.

Protected Games

Each game and press leg is tested on its OWN standing to decide whether it’s protected (settled normally on 18) or pulled into the pot.

FRONT-NINE pieces (the Nassau Front 9 and the front leg of a front-nine press) go into the pot whenever they are NOT EVEN — protected only when dead even.

EVERYTHING ELSE (Back 9, Total 18, back-nine presses, the back leg of a front-nine press) is protected when inside the band, and goes into the pot only when outside it:

  • 2v2: −2, −1, even, +1, +2 (in the pot at ±3 or more)
  • 1v1: −1, even, +1 (in the pot at ±2 or more)

The Pot

Every piece pulled out is consolidated into one pot — the net difference measured from the Leaders’ side (Leaders ahead adds the stake; Trailers ahead subtracts it).

Settling on the 18th Hole

  • Leaders win at least one point outright on 18 — the Leaders win TWICE the pot.
  • The 18th is a draw — the Leaders win the pot ONCE. Protected games pay out as they stood after 17.
  • Trailers win at least one point on 18 — the pot is VOID. This is the “nothing” in double-or-nothing.
Tip: It’s a true double-or-nothing for the Leaders — the protected games still settle normally on top in every case.

In the App

After the 17th hole, the match card shows the Big I offer with the pot size and a side-by-side comparison of “Not called” vs “Called 🌍” for every 18th result. Tap “🌍 Call The International” to engage it (with an Undo before the round is saved). Pieces in the pot are marked with 🌍.

8. The 6-Point Game

A three-player game where 6 points are distributed each hole based on net scores, with handicaps zeroed off the lowest-handicap player among the three.

Result on the holePoints awarded
One player outright lowest net6 to that player, 0 to the other two
Two players tie for lowest3 each to the tied players, 0 to the third
All three tie2 points each

Every hole distributes exactly 6 points; across 18 holes the totals add up to 108. Choose any three players and set the dollar value per point (25¢ to $10). The 6-Point card uses the same Derrick style and ends with a TOTAL PTS row, then a money standing per player (CURRENT $ during the round, $ WON/LOST when complete).

10. Skins

Skins is a hole-by-hole game: each hole is worth one skin. Win the hole outright and you win the skin; tie and the skin carries over, so the pot can build. Any two or more players can play.

Setup

  • Players — tap each player in the Skins game (two or more).
  • Dollars per skin — $1, $2, $5, $10, or $20.
  • Gross or Net — Net uses deck-adjusted handicaps so higher handicaps get strokes.
  • Carry-over — on (the usual way), a tied hole’s skin carries forward to whoever next wins a hole. Off, a tied skin is simply not awarded.

How a Hole Is Won

  • The single lowest score on a hole (gross or net) wins that skin, plus any skins carried from previous tied holes.
  • If two or more tie for lowest, nobody wins the hole; with carry-over on, those skins roll forward.
  • On the 18th, any skins still carrying are decided by that hole, or by the end-of-round rule if it’s tied.

After 18 Holes

  • Void (default) — skins unclaimed after a tied 18th simply vanish.
  • Last hole sweeps — the 18th sweeps the entire pot; if it’s also tied, the app flags a play-off and holds the money until the group settles it.

Settlement

Skins uses a shared-pot settlement — each skin you win pays the per-skin amount from the pool, and everyone contributes their fair share. A player who wins their share comes out even; more than their share is up; none means they pay in. Totals always balance to zero. Tap the 💰 Skins tab for the live card; Skins money also appears in the money summary, share/print, and History.

Tip: With five players at $5 a skin, a clean sweep of a hole with three skins carried in is worth $15 — the carry-over is where Skins gets exciting.

11. Vegas

Vegas is a lively two-against-two team game. Each team’s two scores combine into a single two-digit “number,” and the teams play the difference between their numbers per point. It swings fast.

Players

Vegas is played by exactly four golfers as two teams of two. You can set it up with four or five players:

  • 4 players — pick which of the three pairings forms the two teams.
  • 5 players — pick any two teams of two (the picker shows all combinations, like 2v2 Nassau). The fifth player sits out Vegas and settles $0 for it, while playing all other games normally.

Setup

  • Teams — tap the pairing that sets your two teams.
  • Dollars per point — 25¢, 50¢, $1, $2, or $5.
  • Gross or Net — Net uses deck-adjusted handicaps for the numbers (the flip still keys off gross birdies).
  • Flip on opponent birdie — on by default; see below.

Forming the Number

Each team’s two scores combine low-digit first — a 4 and a 5 become 45. A blow-up runs the digits out: a 4 and a 10 become 410. The lower number wins the hole.

The Flip

With the flip on, a gross birdie or better by the OPPOSING team flips your number so the high digit leads: 45 flips to 54; 410 flips to 104.

  • Only a single flip applies — an eagle does not flip more than a birdie.
  • The flip is triggered by a GROSS birdie even in the net version.
  • Turn the flip off to play straight numbers.

Settlement

Each hole, the difference between the two teams’ numbers is multiplied by the dollars per point; the losing team pays the winning team, both partners sharing the result. Tap the 🎲 Vegas tab for the live card (numbers, flip indicator, points, running total, money). Vegas money also appears in the money summary, share/print, and History.

Tip: A blow-up plus an opponent’s birdie is the classic Vegas disaster — your 4 and 10 become 410, then flip to 104 against the other team’s 34. At a dollar a point that’s a $70 hole.

12. Match Cards

Match Cards lets you play a 1v1 Nassau against one or two golfers in OTHER groups — players you aren’t scoring live. You keep your own scores during your round; your opponents’ scores are entered later in the clubhouse. At the end, a separate ⚔️ Match Cards round is saved to History with your scores filled in and the opponents’ columns blank.

You play a separate 1v1 Nassau against each opponent. Two opponents means two independent matches — your opponents do not play each other.

Setting Up Match Cards

  • Enable Match Cards on the Setup screen (offered whenever there’s at least one player and Scorecard only is off).
  • Tap + Add Opponent and either pick from your saved players or type a name and index directly. Up to two opponents.
  • Each opponent shows their Index, a Tee deck selector, and the resulting Adjusted handicap.
  • If an opponent has a Golf Canada ID, a green Fetch pill appears — tap it to pull their current handicap.
  • Set the Nassau bet per side ($1, $2, $5, or $10) for each opponent.
Tip: The same Match Cards options are available mid-round under ⚙️ Mid-Round Match & Player Settings.

Scoring a Match Cards Round Later

The ⚔️ Match Cards round lands in History with a purple border and a 📝 Score Round pill, listing the participants. Tap Score Round to open it like a live scorecard — your scores filled in, opponents’ blank, starting at the first unscored hole. When complete, it auto-saves back to the same entry (no duplicate). If interrupted, Save & Pause re-saves it with a ▶ Resume Scoring pill.

14. Round History

Tap 📜 Round / History in the Setup action bar to open Round History. (History and Backup are now separate screens — see Section 15 for backups.)

Saving a Round

  • Once all 18 holes are scored, a 💾 Save Round button appears at the top of the Scorecard screen.
  • If you don’t save manually, the round saves automatically when you confirm a New Round.

Viewing Past Rounds

Each entry shows the date, weather, pace of play, score pills for every player, a colour-coded border (green = to handicap, gold = near, red = tough day), and the games played. A ⚔️ Match Cards round shows a purple border. Tap any round for a detailed view: Final Totals, weather, the full scorecard, and each game’s detail card (Nassau, 6-Point, Stableford, Skins, Vegas, Snake).

Editing a History Round

Open a completed round and tap ✏️ Edit. The round loads at hole 1 with all scores editable. Tap 💾 Update History to save, or ↩ Discard Changes to revert. Weather, date, and elapsed time are never modified. You can’t edit history while an active round is in progress.

Paused Rounds — Save & Resume

If interrupted, tap ⚙️ End Round Early → 💾 Save & Pause Round. The partial round is saved and you return to a clean Setup screen. Paused rounds show a ▶ Resume Round button — tap it to reload at the first unscored hole. No games or settings are lost.

Tip: A paused ⚔️ Match Cards round shows a ▶ Resume Scoring pill instead — see Section 12.

Deleting a Round

Tap the ✕ button next to a round and confirm to permanently delete it.

15. Backup & Restore

Your players and round history live only on your phone, so keeping a backup off the device is the one sure way to protect them. Tap 💾 Backup / Data in the Setup action bar to open the Backup & Restore screen — now its own dedicated page, separate from History.

Exporting a Backup

  • 📤 Export Backup — on iPhone this opens the iOS share sheet so you can save to Files or iCloud, or email it to yourself. The file (e.g. “Nassau-Golf-Backup-Jun-25-2026.json”) contains all saved contacts, your full round history, and any round in progress.
  • 📥 Import Backup — reads a previously exported file and restores everything.

The Backup screen also shows how many rounds are stored and, once you’ve exported at least once, when your last backup was taken.

Automatic Safety Copy

After every round you save, the app quietly keeps an extra safety copy of your data on the device — an automatic, behind-the-scenes safeguard. It protects against accidental in-app data loss, but because it’s still on the same phone, it is not a substitute for exporting a real backup off the device.

Backup Reminders

Every couple of rounds, a one-tap Back Up Now banner nudges you to export a fresh backup. It counts rounds since your last export — not total rounds — and stays out of the way while you’re scoring. Tapping it takes you to Export; dismissing it resets the count.

⚠️ iOS can wipe a PWA’s local storage when you remove the Home Screen icon, run low on storage, or in some iOS update scenarios. Export a backup regularly — especially before reinstalling or clearing Safari data.
Truly automatic off-device backup isn’t possible from a web app on iOS — saving a file always needs a tap on the share sheet. The in-app safety copy is the automatic part; the Export is the bit only you can trigger.

17. The Snake 3-Putt Money Game

An optional money game that settles every 3 holes. Whoever held the snake at holes 3, 6, 9, 12, 15, and 18 pays each other participant the bet. The snake resets after each settlement — 6 independent stretches per round.

Setup

  • Check Enable Snake Game in the purple 🐍 card.
  • Choose the bet per player per settlement ($1, $2, $5, $10, or $20).
  • Select Participants — only checked players are in the game.

Tracking During the Round

A compact bar above the player cards lets you record who 3-putted, with the current stretch holder shown (e.g. “H4-6: Bruce”). You can go back and change a past entry.

Settlement

At each settlement hole, the holder pays each other participant the bet. Example: 5 players at $5 — the holder pays $20. If nobody held the snake in a stretch, that settlement is skipped.

The Snake Tab

Tap the 🐍 Snake tab for a Settlement Holes table, Running Totals per player, and the current stretch holder (hidden after hole 18). Snake money is included in the live amounts, the 🐍 Snake $ row, and Total $ Today.

19. Troubleshooting

My round disappeared

Use 💾 Save & Pause Round before ending a round early — it saves to History where you can resume it. A round abandoned without saving cannot be recovered.

The app looks outdated

Tap Update App in the Setup action bar. If that doesn’t do it, clear Safari site data for the app, force-close, and reopen from the Home Screen icon (Section 1).

Pop-ups aren’t appearing

Make sure you’re running from the Home Screen icon, not a Safari tab. Some dialogs only work in standalone (installed) mode.

My player data or history disappeared

This can happen if you removed the Home Screen icon with “Remove App,” which wipes PWA storage on iOS. Restore with 📥 Import Backup if you have a backup. Always export a backup before removing or reinstalling, and never use Remove App.

A player has quit, been injured, or left the round

Tap ⚙️ Mid-Round Match & Player Settings on the Scorecard screen and remove them. In a betting round this also drops them from active games. Their scorecard column remains but they no longer block the group from advancing.

A score or game setting looks wrong

  • Edit a handicap mid-round by tapping the IDX value and ✏️ on the player’s card.
  • Correct scores any time with the scroll wheel — totals, presses, and Birdie Juice recalculate automatically.

The bottom buttons disappeared on the Setup screen

The Setup action bar always reappears when you return to Setup. If it’s ever missing, switch to another screen and back, or force-close and reopen.

Still having trouble?

Take a screenshot showing the screen, the scores, and the version number on the Update App button, and reach out for help.
