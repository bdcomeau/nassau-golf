1 · Installing on Your iPhone

Nassau Golf is a web app that installs to your Home Screen like a regular app and works offline once loaded.

⚠️ You must install the app to your device for your data to be saved reliably. If you only open the web address in a browser tab without adding it to your Home Screen, your saved players, favorites, and round history can be cleared by the browser at any time. Install it first, then always open it from the Home Screen icon — not from a browser tab or bookmark.

How to install on iPhone:

  • Open the app link in Safari on your iPhone (it must be Safari — not Chrome or another browser).
  • Tap the Share button (the square with an arrow pointing up) in the toolbar.
  • Scroll down the Share menu and tap Add to Home Screen.
  • Rename it if you like, then tap Add in the top-right corner.
  • Launch it from the new Home Screen icon — it opens full-screen with no browser bars.
Once installed and opened at least once, the app works without a signal — handy on the course. Live weather and Golf Canada lookups still need a connection when used.
Always launch from the Home Screen icon, not a Safari bookmark. The icon runs in "standalone mode," which is full-screen and is what keeps your data saved on the device.

2 · Setting Up a Round

Adding Players

  • Use the + and − buttons to set the number of players (2 to 5).
  • For each player, enter their name and current handicap index.
  • Optionally, enter their 10-digit Golf Canada Member ID.
  • Tap the 💾 save-disk to store a player's details for quick selection in future rounds.
  • Use the "— saved players —" dropdown to load a saved player into any slot.

As you type each player's name, the match selection buttons below update live to show the actual names instead of "P1," "P2" etc. — so you can see exactly who is matched against whom before starting.

Each golfer must be unique. If the same name (or Golf Canada ID) is entered twice, the duplicate rows are outlined in red and the round won't start until it's fixed.

Favorites — Your Regular Group

Most groups play with the same people every time, so you can save your regulars as Favorites and have them fill in automatically each round.

  1. Save your regular players first using the 💾 disk on each row (or a Golf Canada lookup, which also saves them).
  2. Tap the ⭐ Favorites button at the top of the setup screen.
  3. Star (☆ → ★) up to five players you golf with regularly.
  4. Mark one of them as "Me" — that's you, the scorekeeper. You always go in slot 1. (Marking yourself also stars you automatically.)
  5. Optionally, set each player's default tee deck using the Tee dropdown beside their name. When favorites load, each golfer comes in on their own default deck.

From then on, your favorites pre-fill the player rows automatically — every time you start a New Round, and whenever you open the app fresh. "Me" lands in slot 1, then the other favorites follow, each on their saved default tee deck with the handicap already adjusted.

  • There is also a ⭐ Load My Favorites button on the setup screen to pull them in on demand at any time.
  • Auto-fill only happens when the rows are empty, so it never overwrites names you've already started typing.
  • Everything stays editable — favorites are just a starting point. Adjust the count, swap people in or out, change a deck for a one-off round, or change handicaps as usual.
  • You can have at most five favorites; to add another, unstar one first.
Favorites — including each player's default tee deck — are stored with your saved players, so they're included in your backups.

Looking Up a Handicap from Golf Canada

If a player has a Golf Canada Member ID, you can pull their current handicap index automatically instead of typing it. Each player row has two stacked buttons on the right: the Golf Canada logo (lookup) on top, and the 💾 save disk below.

  • Enter the player's name and their 10-digit Golf Canada Member ID in the ID field.
  • Tap the Golf Canada logo button. The handicap fills in automatically, and the player is saved to your contacts at the same time.

The first time you use the lookup, the app asks once for your Golf Canada username and password; these are saved on your device so you only enter them once.

⚠️ Your Golf Canada password is stored on your device so you don't have to re-enter it each round. It is only ever sent to the lookup service to fetch handicaps. If you'd rather not store it, simply type handicaps in manually instead.

Where to Find the Member Number

On the Golf Canada website, do a Handicap Lookup for the golfer. Their 10-digit Golf Canada Member Number appears just under their name, as highlighted below:

Enter that number in the player's Golf Canada Member Number field on the setup screen, then tap the Golf Canada logo button to fetch the current handicap.

Automatic Handicap Refresh

Once a player has a Golf Canada Member Number saved, the app keeps their handicap current for you. Whenever players are loaded — favorites filling in on a new round, opening the app, or picking a saved player — the app quietly refreshes their handicap in the background.

  • A green line under the Member Number field shows when it was last refreshed, e.g. "🟢 Handicap current as of Jun 18, 13:45."
  • To avoid unnecessary lookups, a handicap is only re-fetched if it's more than about three hours old. Recently-fetched handicaps are left as they are.
  • Auto-refresh is silent — if you're offline or haven't set up your Golf Canada login yet, it simply leaves the existing handicap in place with no error pop-ups.
  • You can always force a fresh lookup at any time by tapping the Golf Canada logo button.
The very first time on a new device you'll set up your Golf Canada login once (by tapping the logo button). After that, the automatic refresh reuses it.

Tee Decks & Handicap Adjustment

Each golfer can select which tee deck they are playing — DECK1, DECK2, DECK3, or DECK4 — from the Tee dropdown on their player row. Because a handicap changes with the tees played, the app adjusts the fetched Golf Canada index for the chosen deck and rounds it to a whole number, which becomes that player's playing handicap for all match calculations.

Tee DeckPlaying handicap (from the fetched Golf Canada index)
DECK1round( index × 121 ÷ 113 + 0.9 )
DECK2round( index × 119 ÷ 113 − 0.4 )
DECK3round( index × 117 ÷ 113 − 2.9 )
DECK4round( index × 109 ÷ 113 − 5.9 )
  • Each player's handicap shows as two stacked boxes: the top box ("Index") is the Golf Canada index in decimals (e.g. 7.5), and the bottom box ("DECK adjusted") is the rounded whole-number playing handicap for the selected deck. The DECK adjusted number is what drives the matches.
  • A note beside the Tee dropdown shows how it was derived, e.g. "DECK1 · from index 7.5 (Golf Canada)."
  • Switching decks recalculates the DECK adjusted box instantly from the index, so you can change tees as many times as you like and the handicap always stays correct.
  • New players default to DECK2. If your whole group plays the same tees, just leave it.
⚠️ A handicap you type in by hand is used exactly as entered — no deck adjustment is applied. (Typing a number also clears any fetched value, so the Tee selector won't change it.) Use the Golf Canada lookup if you want the automatic per-deck adjustment.

Choosing a Starting Hole

For shotgun-start tournaments, use the Starting Hole selector to choose which hole your group tees off on (1–18). All scoring, match calculations, and hole badges throughout the app adjust automatically.

Round Type — Scorecard Only

Below the player list is a "Scorecard only" toggle. Switch it on when you just want to keep score — no Nassau or 6-Point betting. When it's on, the betting-game sections disappear from setup and you can start the round without choosing any games.

  • The 💰 Nassau and 6️⃣ 6-Point tabs are hidden from the bottom navigation.
  • The master scorecard shows no money rows, and Round History skips the money summary and game cards for that round.
  • Everything else still works: scores, handicaps, stroke circles, round timing, live weather, Birdie Juice, and history.
  • On the Scorecard screen the manage button reads "⚙️ Players" and opens a players-only screen (add, remove, or edit handicaps — no game options).
Scorecard-only is chosen per round, so you can keep score casually one day and run a full Nassau the next. The setting resets to off when you start a new round.

"What If You'd Played Nassau?"

In a scorecard-only round, once all 18 holes are scored, a "🤔 What if you'd played Nassau?" banner appears below the master scorecard. Tap it to open a fun calculator that shows what each possible matchup would have paid, based on the day's actual scores — using the full Nassau engine (Front 9 / Back 9 / Total 18 with automatic presses).

  • A "$ per side" selector at the top defaults to $2; tap − / + to change it and every result recalculates instantly.
  • For a four-player group it shows all three 2v2 team splits plus all six head-to-head matchups; with three or five players it shows the head-to-head matchups.
  • Each line reads like "Wayne & Chris win $28" or "All square — $0."
This is purely for fun — no scorecards are drawn, no money is tracked, and nothing is saved. It just answers "what would have happened?"

Choosing Your Games

Set up any combination of games (skip this entirely if "Scorecard only" is on). At least one game (Nassau or 6-Point) is required to start a betting round.

2v2 Nassau Games — Pair players into two-person teams. The bet amount applies separately to Front 9, Back 9, and Total 18. Multiple 2v2 games can run at once.

1v1 Nassau Games — Head-to-head match between any two players, with separate Front 9 / Back 9 / Total 18 bets.

6-Point Game — Choose any three players. Set the dollar value per point (25¢ to $10). Can be played with or without Nassau games.

Resetting the Setup Screen

The "↺ Reset Setup" button (below Start Round) clears all entered names, handicaps, Golf Canada IDs, and selected games, and resets to 2 players. A confirmation appears first. Your saved player contacts are not affected.

Starting the Round

Tap Start Round. The app jumps straight to the Scorecard screen, ready for the first hole.

3 · Scoring — The Scorecard Screen

The Scorecard screen shows one hole at a time, with a card for each player. It's where you'll spend most of the round.

Navigating Between Holes

  • Use the ‹ and › arrows at the top to move between holes in play order.
  • If you tap › (forward) and any player is missing a score on the current hole, the app stays on that hole and shows a red banner: "Missing score for: [name]." Tapping the banner opens the manage screen directly.
  • The hole number, par, and HCP rating are shown at the top of the screen.
  • For shotgun starts, the badge in the top-right shows your match position — e.g. "4th HOLE OF MATCH" if you started on Hole 5 and are now on Hole 8.

Player Cards

Each player card shows their name, handicap index (tap the Idx value and ✏️ to edit mid-round), and their current money total for the round (green = winning, red = losing, gold = even). Once a score is entered for a player on the current hole, their card gains a gold border — a quick visual confirmation that their score is recorded.

The Master Scorecard

Below the player cards, the master scorecard shows all players' gross scores in the authentic Derrick Golf & Winter Club card style — charcoal hole column, olive par row, and the Derrick logo in the corner. Stroke holes are pre-marked with red circles:

MarkMeaning
Thin red circlePlayer gets 1 shot on that hole
Double red circlePlayer gets 2 shots on that hole

Once a gross score is entered, it appears inside the red circle on stroke holes. The card also shows OUT, IN, TOT, and NET rows. NET is each player's gross total minus their full handicap index.

Round Timing

When you start a round, the start time is recorded. The moment the 18th hole's last score is entered, the finish time is stamped and a pace-of-play line appears — e.g. "⏱️ Round started 12:36 PM · Pace of play 4 hours 10 minutes." Editing a score back to incomplete clears the finish time; it re-stamps when you finish again.

Live Weather

A weather strip appears on the Scorecard screen, just below the Front 9 / Back 9 tabs, showing live conditions from a Davis weather station via WeatherLink — for example: "🌡️ 11°C · 💨 calm · 💧 93% · 🌧️ 0 mm today." It refreshes automatically every couple of minutes.

  • Temperature in °C, wind in km/h with direction (or "calm" when still), and humidity.
  • Rainfall: the day's total always shows; when it's actively raining, the current rate is added too (e.g. "🌧️ 3 mm/h (today 7.9 mm)").

The conditions at the start and finish of the round are saved with it. In Round History, a "🌦️ Conditions" card shows the Start and Finish readings.

The first time the weather strip loads, it asks once for the WeatherLink connection details (Worker URL, API Key, API Secret, and Station ID). These save on your device — you only enter them once. Tap the strip any time to re-enter them.

Money Summary

Once all 18 holes are scored, three money rows appear at the bottom of the master scorecard (each player's amount in their own column, green for up, red for down): Nassau $, 6-Point $ (only if there are 6-Point games), and Total $ Today in bold. These don't appear in scorecard-only rounds.

4 · The Score Entry Wheel

Each player card features a drum-roll style score selector. Tapping the centre box sets par. The ◀ and ▶ arrows adjust the score one stroke at a time, and you can also swipe up or down on the centre box. The recorded score highlights brighter on the side panels so you can confirm at a glance.

Eagle and worse-than-triple scores are still reachable using the arrow buttons — the side panels just show the most common outcomes for speed.

5 · The Nassau Tab

Tap the 💰 Nassau icon at the bottom to see live match scorecards for every Nassau game.

Reading a Match Scorecard

Each Nassau match is shown as a Derrick-style card with the first three columns (Hole, HCP, Par), then each player's gross scores, then running columns for every bet: Nassau F9, Nassau B9, Nassau 18 (shown vertically with a light tint to set them apart), and each press (P1, P2, …).

  • Player handicaps here are the ADJUSTED match handicaps — the lowest handicap in the match drops to scratch (0) and the others play off that.
  • Stroke holes are pre-marked with red circles using those adjusted handicaps.
  • Each bet column shows the running match state per hole: E (even), +1/−2 (holes up or down), or P where a press begins.
  • After the 17th hole, a 🌍 globe marks any bet eligible for The International (see Section 6).

Presses

A press is an extra bet worth DOUBLE the Nassau amount. A new press starts automatically the moment the parent game first reaches 2 up or 2 down: the trigger hole shows "P" in a new column, and the press begins counting on the very next hole.

  • P1 starts when the overall 18 game first hits ±2; P2 starts when P1 hits ±2; and so on.
  • A press started in the first nine settles in TWO halves: the first half settles on its standing at the 9th hole; the second half on its cumulative standing at the 18th. Each half is worth the press amount.
  • A press started in the back nine is a single bet, settled at the 18th hole.
  • After 18 holes, any bet that is not even pays out — a final margin of ±1, ±2, or ±3 all settle for the bet's amount.

Result Banner & WINS Row

Above the WINS row, a gold banner shows the match standing. While the round is in progress it reads "… is currently ahead by $X" (or "… are currently ahead by $X" for a 2v2) — nobody has won until the 18th hole is complete. Once all 18 holes are scored, it switches to the final result, e.g. "Greg Hines wins $38" or "Bruce & Greg win $24," or "All square — $0."

The WINS row below shows each bet column's settlement from the first listed side's (Player 1 / Side A) perspective: a win is a positive amount, a loss a red negative amount, a halved bet $0, and a bet folded into the International as 🌍.

6 · The International ("The Big I")

The International — "The Big I" — is an optional side bet available only after the 17th hole of an 18-hole Nassau is complete. The trailing side may ask for it; the leading side accepts or declines. It's agreed verbally at the tee, then the scorekeeper engages it in the app.

Leaders and Trailers

The side that is UP in total money after 17 holes are the Leaders; the other side are the Trailers. (Based on the overall money standing, so it's always clear-cut.)

Protected Games

Any game — Front 9, Back 9, Total 18, or any Press — whose standing is INSIDE the protected band is "protected": it's left out of the Big I and settles normally on the 18th as if the Big I never happened. The bands are:

  • 2v2: −2, −1, even, +1, +2
  • 1v1: −1, even, +1

Each piece is judged on its OWN standing. A front-nine press is judged as two independent halves: the front half (through hole 9) and the back half (through hole 17). For example, if a press is +4 after nine holes but even after 17, the front half is out of band (goes into the pot) while the back half is protected and plays out normally.

The Pot

Every NON-protected game (and press half) — anything outside the band, i.e. 2v2 at ±3 or more, 1v1 at ±2 or more — is pulled out of normal settlement and consolidated into one pot. The pot is the NET difference between the two sides on those games.

Example: if the non-protected games tallied $20 for the Leaders and $2 for the Trailers, the pot would be the $18 difference. Those games no longer settle on their own — their value lives entirely in the pot.

Settling on the 18th Hole

The 18th hole decides the pot, based on the Leaders' points on the hole (in a 2v2 the hole is the usual high-low, worth up to two points; in a 1v1 it's a single point). Protected games settle normally on top in every case:

  • Leaders win at least one point on 18 (net ahead on the hole) → Leaders win twice the pot.
  • The 18th is a draw — both balls halved, or a 1–1 split where each side wins one ball → Leaders win the pot once. Protected games pay out as they stood after 17.
  • Trailers win at least one point on 18 (net ahead on the hole) → Trailers win the pot once.
Note the asymmetry that makes it a gamble for the Trailers to call: the Leaders double the pot by winning the 18th, but the Trailers only win a single pot by winning it.

In the App

  • After the 17th hole, the Nassau match card shows the Big I offer for the trailing side. It lists the pot size and a comparison table of how the match settles for every 18th-hole result — "Not called" versus "Called 🌍" side by side.
  • In that table, amounts are shown from the trailing side's point of view (+ means they win, − means they pay), and rows where calling beats not calling for them are highlighted — so the gamble is easy to read at a glance.
  • Tap "🌍 Call The International" to engage it (after both sides agree verbally). The card then shows a simple "🌍 International has been Called!" confirmation with an Undo button to reverse it before the round is saved.
  • Games in the pot are marked with a 🌍 globe; protected games show their normal running totals.
  • Once the 18th is scored, the card shows the final Big I result and the overall match total.

7 · The 6-Point Game

A three-player game where 6 points are distributed each hole based on net scores, with handicaps zeroed off the lowest-handicap player among the three.

Result on the holePoints awarded
One player has the outright lowest net score4 to that player, 0 to the other two
Two players tie for lowest net3 each to the tied players, 0 to the third
All three players tie2 points each

Every hole distributes exactly 6 points. Across 18 holes the three players' totals always add up to 108.

Tap the 6️⃣ icon at the bottom to see the 6-Point card. It shows the players' gross scores (with red stroke circles), their points columns, and a TOTAL PTS row. Below that is the money standing per player: while the round is in progress this is a "CURRENT $" row — each player's winnings based on the points so far, calculated the same way as the final settlement. Once all 18 holes are scored it becomes the final "$ WON/LOST" row.

8 · Birdie Juice

Birdie Juice is a fun running tally of birdies (and better) made during the round. Tap the 🐦🥃 icon to see who has earned what. A badge on the icon shows the current count. It's a celebratory side-tracker — it doesn't affect any money settlement.

9 · Round History & Backup

Viewing Past Rounds

Tap the 📜 History icon to see all saved rounds. Tap any round for a detailed read-only view showing the Final Totals, a "🌦️ Conditions" card with the start and finish weather (when captured), the full scorecard, Nassau match cards, and 6-Point results. (Money and game cards are skipped for scorecard-only rounds.)

Backup & Restore

At the top of the History screen, a Backup & Restore card provides two buttons:

  • 📤 Export Backup — downloads a nassau-backup-YYYY-MM-DD.json file to your iPhone's Files app. Contains all saved player contacts, round history, and the current round in progress.
  • 📥 Import Backup — reads a previously exported backup file and restores everything.
⚠️ iOS can wipe a web app's local storage when you remove the Home Screen icon, run low on device storage, or in some iOS update scenarios. Export a backup regularly — especially before reinstalling or clearing Safari data. A backup after each round takes seconds.

Deleting a Round

From the History list, tap the 🗑️ icon next to a round and confirm to permanently delete it.

10 · Starting a New Round

Tap the 🔄 New Round icon. Because this erases the current round, it asks you to confirm with a swipe-to-erase slider. After confirming, you're returned to a clean Setup screen. Tip: export a backup or make sure the current round is saved to History first.

11 · Troubleshooting

A player has quit, been injured, or left the round

Tap ⚙️ Matches (or ⚙️ Players in a scorecard-only round) at the top of the Scorecard screen to open the manage screen. Remove them there; in a betting round this also drops them from any active Nassau and 6-Point games. Their scorecard column remains (with scores already entered) but they'll no longer block the group from advancing holes or settling bets.

The missing-score warning banner is also tappable — it opens the manage screen directly.

The weather strip says "unavailable"

Tap the strip to re-enter the WeatherLink connection details. A connection is required for live weather; the rest of the app works offline.

A handicap lookup failed

Check the 10-digit Golf Canada Member ID and that your Golf Canada login is correct. You can always type a handicap in manually instead.
