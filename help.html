Privacy & Data Security

Everything stays on your device

All round data, player names, handicaps, betting results, and Golf Canada member IDs are stored exclusively in your iPhone’s browser storage (localStorage). This data never leaves your device and is never sent to any server, cloud service, or third party. There is no Nassau Golf database or backend — the app is a single file that runs entirely on your phone.

Golf Canada Member ID & Password

When you enter your Golf Canada credentials (member ID and password), they are used for one purpose only: fetching your current handicap index directly from Golf Canada’s API. Your credentials are:

  • Stored only in your device’s local browser storage
  • Never transmitted to any server other than Golf Canada’s own secure API
  • Never shared with other players, the app developer, or any third party
  • Never included in round history exports or backups

Once saved, your handicap refreshes automatically every 3 hours in the background. Your credentials are read locally each time and sent directly to Golf Canada. No one else ever sees them.

How long credentials are remembered

Your Golf Canada credentials persist indefinitely and survive app updates, closing the app, and restarting your phone. They are only lost if you manually clear Safari website data in iPhone Settings, or set up a brand new iPhone without restoring from an iCloud backup.

Weather data

The app fetches live weather from the Derrick GWC weather station via a secure Cloudflare Worker proxy. No personal information is sent with this request — it is a read-only call to a weather instrument.

Need help with Golf Canada credentials?

If you don’t have a Golf Canada account or have forgotten your credentials, contact Trevor Goplin, Golf Professional at Derrick Golf & Winter Club. He can look up your member ID and assist with account setup at golfcanada.ca.

🔒 Nassau Golf does not collect analytics, does not serve ads, has no user accounts, and has no server. Your data is yours alone.

Round, Match & Player Settings

Tap the ⚙️ Round, Match & Player Settings button on the score entry screen to access mid-round adjustments. All settings are collapsible — tap any section header to expand it.

1. ⚡ Auto-Advance Scoring

Always visible at the top. Toggle automatic hole progression on or off mid-round without restarting. When ON, the app moves to the next hole automatically once all players have a score entered.

2. 🚩 Change Starting Hole

If you selected the wrong starting hole, fix it here. All scores already entered shift to the correct holes automatically and all matches recalculate. Works even if scores wrap past hole 18 back to hole 1.

3. 🏌️ Players — Tee Deck Change

Each player row shows their current handicap index (display-only) and a Tee Deck selector. Select a different deck and the handicap recalculates automatically. All Nassau and 6-Point matches update instantly.

  • Golf Canada players — recalculates from their stored Golf Canada baseline index
  • Manually-entered players — uses the index entered at setup as the baseline

The handicap field is display-only and cannot be edited directly — change it by selecting a new Tee Deck.

Adding & Removing Players

Within the Players section you can also add or remove players mid-round:

  • Add Player — if fewer than 5 players are in the round, a + Add Player button appears at the bottom of the Players section. The new player starts with no scores and is not automatically added to any existing Nassau or 6-Point matches — add them manually using the match sections below.
  • Remove Player — tap the ✕ button beside any player to remove them. Their scores are deleted and they are removed from all active Nassau and 6-Point matches. This cannot be undone, so use with care.

4. 2v2 Nassau Games

Tap a pairing to add or remove it from the active round. Adding a match starts it from the current hole.

5. 1v1 Nassau Games

Tap a matchup to add or remove it. Removing a match keeps scores but drops it from Nassau totals.

6. 🎯 6-Point Game

Pick any 3 players to add or remove the 6-Point game. Player names reflect the actual golfers in the round.

7. 🐍 Snake 3-Putt Game

Turn the Snake game on or off, adjust the bet amount, and manage which players participate mid-round.

8. ⚔️ Match Cards

Enable or adjust Match Cards mid-round. Add up to two opponents from your saved players, set each opponent’s tee deck and Nassau bet, and use the Golf Canada Fetch pill to pull a current handicap. The separate ⚔️ Match Cards round is still saved at the end of the round for scoring later (see Section 9).

1. Installing Nassau Golf on Your iPhone

Nassau Golf is a Progressive Web App (PWA). It is not downloaded from the App Store — instead, you add it to your Home Screen directly from Safari. Once installed, it opens full-screen like any other app, works without an internet connection during your round, and keeps your round data saved on your device.

⚠️ You MUST install the app to your device before your data will be reliably saved. If you only open the web address in a browser tab (without adding it to your Home Screen / installing it), your saved players, favorites, and round history can be cleared by the browser at any time. Install it first, then always open it from the installed icon — not from a browser tab or bookmark.

⚠️ Important: Do NOT use the “Remove App” option on the Home Screen icon — on iOS this wipes the app’s local data including your saved players and round history. To update or reinstall, use the Safari method described below instead.

Step-by-Step Installation

  • Open Safari on your iPhone (the installation must be done in Safari — it will not work correctly in Chrome or other browsers).
  • Go to the Nassau Golf web address:
  • Once the page loads, tap the Share button — the square icon with an arrow pointing up, located in the bottom toolbar (or top toolbar on some iPhones).
  • Scroll down the Share menu and tap “Add to Home Screen.”
  • You can rename the app if you like, then tap “Add” in the top-right corner.
  • A Nassau Golf icon will now appear on your Home Screen, just like any other app.

Tip: Always launch the app from the Home Screen icon, not from a Safari bookmark. The Home Screen icon runs in “standalone mode,” which gives you a full-screen experience and is required for the app to work correctly.

Keeping the App Current

Nassau Golf updates automatically. When a new version is published, the app downloads it in the background the next time you open it with an internet connection and applies it on the following launch.

If you think you might be on an old version, check the small version number at the bottom of the Setup screen. To get the latest version, try these steps in order:

  • Force-close and reopen — swipe the app up in the iOS app switcher to close it completely, then tap the Home Screen icon. This is usually all it takes.
  • Force-close while online — same as above, but make sure you have a Wi-Fi or cell signal before reopening so the latest version can download.
  • Clear Safari site data — go to Settings → Safari → Advanced → Website Data, search for bdcomeau.github.io, swipe left on it and tap Delete. Force-close and reopen. This clears the cached old version so the fresh one loads. This does NOT delete your saved players or round history.

⚠️ Do NOT delete the Home Screen icon and reinstall the app. On iOS, removing a PWA icon permanently erases all its local data including your saved players and round history. Always use the force-close or Safari data methods above.

Protecting Your Data

Your saved players, favorites, and round history live in local storage on your device, which is tied to the installed app. This is exactly why installing to your Home Screen matters — an installed app holds onto its data, while a plain browser tab can have it cleared without warning. As an extra safeguard, back up regularly using the Export Backup button in the History tab. A backup takes seconds and protects you from data loss if your device ever clears the storage.

2. Setting Up a Round

When you open Nassau Golf, you’ll land on the Setup screen. This is where you add players, set handicaps, choose your starting hole, and configure the games for the round.

Adding Players

  • Use the + and − buttons to set the number of players (1 to 5). A fresh setup screen starts with a single player — you (the “Me” favorite).
  • For each player, enter their name and current handicap index.
  • Optionally, enter their 10-digit Golf Canada Member ID (must be exactly 10 digits if provided).
  • Each player row has three action pills on the right: a Golf Canada Fetch pill (logo + “Fetch”), a Save pill (💾 + “Save”), and — once the row has a name or ID — a Clear pill (✕ + “Clear”).
  • Tap Save to store a player’s details for quick selection in future rounds.
  • Use the “— saved players —” dropdown to load a saved player into any slot.

Tip: As you type each player’s name, the match selection buttons below update live to show the actual names instead of “P1,” “P2” etc. — so you can see exactly who is matched against whom before starting.

Tip: Each golfer must be unique. If the same name (or Golf Canada ID) is entered twice, the duplicate rows are outlined in red and the round won’t start until it’s fixed.

Favorites — Your Regular Group

Most groups play with the same people every time, so you can save your regulars as Favorites and have them fill in automatically each round.

  • Save your regular players first using the 💾 disk on each row (or a Golf Canada lookup, which also saves them).
  • Tap the ⭐ Favorites button at the top of the setup screen.
  • Star (☆ → ★) up to five players you golf with regularly.
  • Mark one of them as “Me” — that’s you, the scorekeeper. You always go in slot 1. (Marking yourself also stars you automatically.)
  • Optionally, set each player’s default tee deck using the Tee dropdown beside their name. When favorites load, each golfer comes in on their own default deck.

From then on, your favorites pre-fill the player rows automatically — every time you start a New Round, and whenever you open the app fresh. “Me” lands in slot 1, then the other favorites follow, each on their saved default tee deck with the handicap already adjusted.

  • There is also a ⭐ Load My Favorites button on the setup screen to pull them in on demand at any time. If one contact is marked as “Me” in your favorites, they are automatically placed in the Player 1 slot on every fresh setup screen and after Reset Setup — so you never have to enter yourself manually.
  • Auto-fill only happens when the rows are empty, so it never overwrites names you’ve already started typing.
  • Everything stays editable — favorites are just a starting point. Adjust the count, swap people in or out, change a deck for a one-off round, or change handicaps as usual.
  • You can have at most five favorites; to add another, unstar one first.

Tip: Favorites — including each player’s default tee deck — are stored with your saved players, so they are included in your backups (Section 11).

Looking Up a Handicap from Golf Canada

If a player has a Golf Canada Member ID, you can pull their current handicap index automatically instead of typing it. Each player row has a green Golf Canada Fetch pill (the Golf Canada logo with the word “Fetch”) in the button column on the right.

  • Enter the player’s name and their 10-digit Golf Canada Member ID in the ID field.
  • Tap the Fetch pill. The handicap fills in automatically, and the player is saved to your contacts at the same time.

Tip: A successful lookup saves the player automatically — no need to tap the Save pill separately. If the player already exists in your contacts, their handicap is simply updated in place (no duplicate). The Save pill is still there for saving players you enter by hand.

⚠️ Your Golf Canada password is stored on your device so you don’t have to re-enter it each round. It is only ever sent to the lookup service to fetch handicaps. If you’d rather not store it, simply type handicaps in manually instead.

What You Need for Live Handicap Fetching

To automatically fetch a player's current handicap index from Golf Canada, you need two things:

  • Golf Canada Member ID — a 10-digit number assigned to every Golf Canada member
  • Golf Canada account credentials — the player must have an account on golfcanada.ca with their member ID linked

If a player doesn't have their credentials or isn't sure, the easiest way to get set up is to contact your club's golf professional. At Derrick Golf & Winter Club, contact Trevor Goplin — he can look up member IDs and assist with Golf Canada account registration.

Alternatively, members can visit golfcanada.ca, click Sign In, and use Forgot Password if they have an existing account, or Register to create a new one and link their member number.

Once a player's member ID is saved and their handicap has been fetched once, it refreshes automatically every 3 hours — no credentials needed again.

Where to Find the Member Number

On the Golf Canada website, do a Handicap Lookup for the golfer. Their 10-digit Golf Canada Member Number appears just under their name, as highlighted below:

Enter that number in the player’s Golf Canada Member Number field on the setup screen, then tap the Golf Canada Fetch pill to fetch the current handicap.

Automatic Handicap Refresh

Once a player has a Golf Canada Member Number saved, the app keeps their handicap current for you. Whenever players are loaded — favorites filling in on a new round, opening the app, or picking a saved player — the app quietly refreshes their handicap in the background.

  • A green line under the Member Number field shows when it was last refreshed, e.g. “🟢 Handicap current as of Jun 18, 13:45.”
  • To avoid unnecessary lookups, a handicap is only re-fetched if it is more than about three hours old. Recently-fetched handicaps are left as they are.
  • Auto-refresh is silent — if you’re offline or haven’t set up your Golf Canada login yet, it simply leaves the existing handicap in place with no error pop-ups.
  • You can always force a fresh lookup at any time by tapping the Golf Canada Fetch pill.

Tip: The very first time on a new device you’ll set up your Golf Canada login once (by tapping the Fetch pill). After that, the automatic refresh reuses it.

Tee Decks & Handicap Adjustment

Each golfer can select which tee deck they are playing from the Tee dropdown on their player row — DECK1, DECK1/2, DECK2, DECK2/3, DECK3, DECK3/4, or DECK4, listed longest to shortest with the combined decks slotted between. Because a handicap changes with the tees played, the app adjusts the fetched Golf Canada index for the chosen deck and rounds it to a whole number, which becomes that player’s playing handicap for all match calculations.

Tee Deck Playing handicap (from the fetched Golf Canada index)
DECK1 round( index × 121 ÷ 113 + 0.9 )
DECK1/2 round( index × 120 ÷ 113 + 0.2 )
DECK2 round( index × 119 ÷ 113 − 0.4 )
DECK2/3 round( index × 118 ÷ 113 − 1.8 )
DECK3 round( index × 117 ÷ 113 − 2.9 )
DECK3/4 round( index × 109 ÷ 113 − 4.9 )
DECK4 round( index × 109 ÷ 113 − 5.9 )
  • Each player’s handicap shows as two stacked boxes: the top box (tagged IDX) is the Golf Canada index in decimals (e.g. 7.5), and the bottom box (tagged ADJ) is the rounded whole-number playing handicap for the selected deck. The ADJ number is what drives the matches.
  • Switching decks recalculates the ADJ box instantly from the index, so you can change tees as many times as you like and the handicap always stays correct.
  • The tee deck is chosen from the deck selector at the bottom of each player card. New players default to DECK2. If your whole group plays the same tees, just leave it.

⚠️ A handicap you type in by hand is used exactly as entered — no deck adjustment is applied. (Typing a number also clears any fetched value, so the Tee selector won’t change it.) Use the Golf Canada lookup if you want the automatic per-deck adjustment.

Choosing a Starting Hole

For shotgun-start tournaments, use the Starting Hole selector to choose which hole your group tees off on (1–18). All scoring, match calculations, and hole badges throughout the app adjust automatically.

Round Type — Scorecard Only

Below the player list is a “Scorecard only” toggle. Switch it on when you just want to keep score — no betting games at all. When it’s on, every game section disappears from setup (Nassau, 6-Point, Snake, Stableford, and Match Cards) and you can start the round without choosing any games. Turning on Scorecard only also clears any Match Cards selection.

  • The 💰 Nassau and 6️⃣ 6-Point tabs are hidden from the bottom navigation.
  • The master scorecard shows no money rows, and Round History skips the money summary and game cards for that round.
  • Everything else still works: scores, handicaps, stroke circles, round timing, live weather, Birdie Juice, and history.
  • On the Scorecard screen the manage button reads “⚙️ Players” and opens a players-only screen (add, remove, or edit handicaps — no game options).

Tip: Scorecard-only is chosen per round, so you can keep score casually one day and run a full Nassau the next. The setting resets to off when you start a new round.

“What If You’d Played Nassau?”

In a scorecard-only round, once all 18 holes are scored, a “🤔 What if you’d played Nassau?” banner appears below the master scorecard. Tap it to open a fun calculator that shows what each possible matchup would have paid, based on the day’s actual scores — using the full Nassau engine (Front 9 / Back 9 / Total 18 with automatic presses).

  • A “$ per side” selector at the top defaults to $2; tap − / + to change it and every result recalculates instantly.
  • For a four-player group it shows all three 2v2 team splits plus all six head-to-head matchups; with three or five players it shows the head-to-head matchups.
  • Each line reads like “Wayne & Chris win $28” or “All square — $0.”

Tip: This is purely for fun — no scorecards are drawn, no money is tracked, and nothing is saved. It just answers “what would have happened?”

Choosing Your Games

Set up any combination of games (skip this entirely if “Scorecard only” is on). The games offered depend on how many players are in the round, so you only ever see games you can actually play:

  • 1 player: only Match Cards is offered (see Section 9). Nassau, 6-Point, Snake, and Stableford are hidden until you add a second player.
  • 2 players: 1v1 Nassau, Snake, Stableford, and Match Cards. 2v2 Nassau (needs 4) and 6-Point (needs 3) are hidden.
  • 3 players: the above plus 6-Point. 2v2 Nassau is still hidden.
  • 4–5 players: every game is available, including 2v2 Nassau.

For a multi-player betting round, at least one game (Nassau, 6-Point, or Snake) is required to start — or switch on Scorecard only.

2v2 Nassau Games

  • Pair players into two-person teams. The bet amount applies separately to Front 9, Back 9, and Total 18.
  • Multiple 2v2 games can run simultaneously.

1v1 Nassau Games

  • Head-to-head match between any two players, with separate Front 9 / Back 9 / Total 18 bets.

6-Point Game

  • Choose any three players. Set the dollar value per point (25¢ to $10).
  • Can be played with or without Nassau games.

Snake 3-Putt Game

An optional side game that can run alongside any Nassau or 6-Point game, or on its own in a scorecard-only round. Enable it with the checkbox in the purple Snake 3-Putt Game card on the Setup screen (between Round Type and 2v2 Nassau). See Section 13 for full details.

  • Whoever last 3-putted holds the snake.
  • At the end of the round, whoever is holding it loses.
  • No money settlement — purely a fun side consequence.
  • Tracked hole by hole on the score entry screen with a compact dropdown.

Managing and Deleting Players

Tap ⭐ Manage Favorites & Players at the top of the Setup screen to open the full player list. Here you can star/unstar players, set default tee decks, mark yourself as “Me,” and remove players permanently by tapping ✕ beside their name. A confirmation dialog appears before deletion.

Golf Canada ID Lock

When a player is loaded from the saved player dropdown or from favorites, and they already have a Golf Canada Member ID on file, the ID field shows as locked (greyed out with a 🔒 lock icon) to prevent accidental edits. Tap 🔒 to unlock it if you genuinely need to change the ID.

Clearing a Player Row

If you need to replace a loaded player with someone new, tap the red ✕ Clear pill in the button column on the right side of the player row. This wipes the name, GC ID, and handicap for that row, leaving it blank and ready for a new entry.

Duplicate Prevention

The “— saved players —” dropdown greys out and disables any player already assigned to another active row, so you cannot accidentally add the same person twice.

Pre-Loaded Player List

Nassau Golf comes pre-loaded with the regular Derrick GWC group — all 24 players with their Golf Canada Member IDs. They appear automatically in the dropdown on first install, and are merged in on every launch without overwriting any data you’ve added.

Score Entry Options

The ⚙️ Score Entry Options card (between Round Type and Snake) contains an Auto-advance holes toggle. When on (default), the app moves to the next hole automatically after all scores are entered. Turn it off to require a manual Next Hole → tap.

Solo Mode

A fresh setup screen starts with a single player — you, the “Me” favorite. With one player, the live round keeps score only (no live Nassau, 6-Point, or Snake on the scorecard), but Match Cards is available: you can pick 1–2 opponents from other groups and a separate ⚔️ Match Cards round is saved at the end for you to score later (see Section 9). The round saves to History like any other. Add a second player at any time to unlock the full set of betting games.

Resetting the Setup Screen

The “↺ Reset Setup” button (below Start Round) clears all entered names, handicaps, Golf Canada IDs, and selected games, and resets to a single player (your “Me” favorite). A confirmation prompt appears before anything is cleared. Your saved player contacts are not affected.

Starting the Round

Tap Start Round. The app jumps straight to the Scorecard screen, ready for the first hole.

3. Scoring — The Scorecard Screen

The Scorecard screen shows one hole at a time, with a card for each player. It is where you’ll spend most of the round.

Navigating Between Holes

  • Use the ‹ and › arrows at the top to move between holes in play order.
  • If you tap › (forward) and any player is missing a score on the current hole, the app will stay on that hole and show a red banner: “Missing score for: [name].” Tapping the banner opens the Manage Matches screen directly.
  • The hole number, par, and HCP rating are shown at the top of the screen.
  • For shotgun starts, the badge in the top-right shows your match position — e.g. “4th HOLE OF MATCH” if you started on Hole 5 and are now on Hole 8.
  • The Scorecard icon at the bottom of the screen shows a gold badge with the same play-order number.

Player Cards

Each player card shows the player’s name and a live combined money total covering all active games — Nassau (1v1 and 2v2), 6-Point, and Snake — in real time (green = winning, red = losing, gold = even). The handicap index and ✏️ edit button sit on the right side of the header beside the money amount, away from the score entry area below — tap it to edit the handicap mid-round. Rounds auto-save to History the moment the last score on hole 18 is entered.

Once a score has been entered for a player on the current hole, their card gains a gold border — a quick visual confirmation that their score is recorded. Cards without a border still need a score entered.

The Master Scorecard

Below the player cards, the master scorecard shows all players’ gross scores in the authentic Derrick Golf & Winter Club card style — charcoal hole column, olive par row, and condensed columns. It uses each player’s FULL handicap.

Once scores are entered, they appear with traditional golf score markings:

Mark Meaning
Green bold ACE Hole in one
Double red circle Eagle or better
Single red circle Birdie
Plain number Par
Single black square Bogey
Double black square Double bogey or worse

The card also shows OUT, IN, TOT, and NET rows. NET is each player’s gross total minus their full handicap index.

The 📤 Share / Print Scorecard button above the master scorecard opens the full round as a clean page in a new browser tab. From there, tap 🖨️ Print / Save PDF to print or save as PDF — or use the iOS Share sheet (tap the share icon in Safari) to send via Messages, Mail, AirDrop, or any other app.

Round Timing

When you start a round, the start time is recorded. The moment the 18th hole’s last score is entered, the finish time is stamped and a pace-of-play line appears at the bottom of the cards — for example: “⏱️ Round started 12:36 PM · Pace of play 4 hours 10 minutes.” If you edit a score back to incomplete, the finish time clears and re-stamps when you finish again.

Live Weather

A weather strip appears on the score entry screen, just below the Front 9 / Back 9 tabs, showing live conditions from a Davis weather station via WeatherLink — for example: “🌡️ 11°C · 💨 calm · 💧 93% · 🌧️ 0 mm today.” It refreshes automatically every couple of minutes while you play.

  • Temperature in °C, wind in km/h with direction (or “calm” when still), and humidity.
  • Rainfall: the day’s total always shows; when it’s actively raining, the current rate is added too (e.g. “🌧️ 3 mm/h (today 7.9 mm)”).

The conditions at the start and finish of the round are saved with it. In Round History, a “🌦️ Conditions” card shows the Start and Finish readings for that round.

Tip: Weather is pre-configured and connects automatically — no setup required. Tap the weather strip any time to refresh conditions manually.

Money Summary

Once all 18 holes are scored, three money rows appear at the bottom of the master scorecard, with each player’s amount in their own column (green for up, red for down):

  • Nassau $ — each player’s Nassau winnings or losses.
  • 6-Point $ — shown only if there are 6-Point games in the round.
  • Total $ Today — the combined total, in bold.

4. The Score Entry Wheel

Each player card features a drum-roll style score selector. The layout is fixed on every hole:

Position What it shows
Far left Birdie (par −1) — always
Centre (unset) Par number in grey — tap to confirm par
Centre (set) Current score with coloured box and label
Right side Bogey (par+1), Double (par+2), Triple (par+3)

Entering a Score

  • When a hole is first displayed, the centre shows the par number in light grey. Tap the centre to confirm par — it immediately lights up in green. The label below the score updates dynamically: Ace (any gross score of 1), Albatross (par 5, 3-under), Eagle, Birdie, Par, Bogey, Double, Triple, Quad, and Moose (score of 10).
  • Tap the centre to confirm par — it immediately lights up in green.
  • Tap any number on the left or right to jump directly to that score.
  • Use the ◀ and ▶ arrows to adjust the score one stroke at a time. Pressing an arrow from the unset state goes directly to par−1 (birdie) or par+1 (bogey).
  • You can also swipe up or down on the centre score box to adjust.
  • The currently recorded score highlights brighter on the side panels so you can confirm at a glance what’s been entered.

Tip: Eagle and worse-than-triple scores are still reachable using the arrow buttons — the side panels just show the most common outcomes for speed.

5. The Nassau Tab

Tap the 💰 Nassau icon at the bottom to see live match scorecards for every Nassau game.

Reading a Match Scorecard

Each Nassau match is shown as a Derrick-style card with the first three columns (Hole, HCP, Par), then each player’s gross scores, then running columns for every bet: Nassau F9, Nassau B9, Nassau 18 (shown vertically with a light tint to set them apart from the presses), and each press (P1, P2, …).

  • Player handicaps here are the ADJUSTED match handicaps — the lowest handicap in the match drops to scratch (0) and the others play off that. The adjusted figures are noted above the card.
  • Stroke holes are pre-marked with red circles (thin = 1 shot, double = 2 shots) using those adjusted handicaps.
  • Each bet column shows the running match state per hole: E (even), +1/−2 (holes up or down), or P where a press begins.
  • After the 17th hole, a 🌍 globe marks any bet that is eligible for The International (see Section 6).

Presses

A press is an extra bet worth DOUBLE the Nassau amount. A new press starts automatically the moment the parent game first reaches 2 up or 2 down: the trigger hole shows “P” in a new column, and the press begins counting on the very next hole.

  • P1 starts when the overall 18 game first hits ±2; P2 starts when P1 first hits ±2; P3 when P2 does; and so on.
  • A press started in the first nine holes settles in TWO halves: the first half settles on its standing at the 9th hole; the second half settles on its cumulative standing at the 18th hole. Each half is worth the press amount.
  • A press started in the back nine is a single bet, settled at the 18th hole.
  • After 18 holes, any bet that is not even pays out — a final margin of ±1, ±2, or ±3 all settle for the bet’s amount.

Result Banner & WINS Row

Once all 18 holes are scored, a gold banner shows the final match result — e.g. “Greg Hines wins $38” (1v1) or “Bruce & Greg win $24” (2v2), or “All square — $0.” The amount combines every settled bet and press (and the International result, if one was called). While the round is still in progress no running total is shown — nobody has won until the 18th hole is complete, so check the WINS row and the per-game columns to see where each game stands. (If the International has been called, the banner shows its status instead.)

The WINS row below shows each bet column’s settlement from the first listed side’s (Player 1 / Side A) perspective: a win shows as a positive dollar amount, a loss as a red negative amount, a halved bet as $0, and a bet folded into the International as 🌍.

6. The International (“The Big I”)

The International — “The Big I” — is an optional side bet available only after the 17th hole of an 18-hole Nassau is complete. The trailing side may ask for it; the leading side accepts or declines. It is agreed verbally at the tee, then the scorekeeper engages it in the app.

Leaders and Trailers

The side that is UP in total money after 17 holes are the Leaders; the other side are the Trailers. (This is based on the overall money standing, so it is always clear-cut.)

Protected Games

Each game and each press leg is tested on its OWN standing to decide whether it is “protected” (left out of the Big I and settled normally on the 18th hole) or pulled into the pot. There are two rules, depending on whether the piece is a front-nine piece or not.

FRONT-NINE pieces — the Nassau Front 9, and the front leg (through hole 9) of any press started on the front nine — go into the pot whenever they are NOT EVEN. If anyone is ahead, even by a single hole, the piece goes in the pot. It is protected only when it is dead even. The band cushion below does NOT apply to these.

EVERYTHING ELSE — the Back 9, Total 18, back-nine presses, and the back leg (through hole 17) of a front-nine press — is protected when its standing is INSIDE the band, and goes into the pot only when it is outside the band. The bands are:

  • 2v2: −2, −1, even, +1, +2 (in the pot at ±3 or more)
  • 1v1: −1, even, +1 (in the pot at ±2 or more)

A front-nine press is judged as two independent halves: the front half (through hole 9) follows the front-nine rule above (in the pot unless even), and the back half (through hole 17) follows the band rule. For example, a press that is +1 after nine holes but even after 17 has its front half in the pot (it isn’t even) while its back half is protected.

The Pot

Every game and press leg pulled out of normal settlement is consolidated into one pot. The pot is the NET difference between the two sides on those pieces, measured from the Leaders’ side: a piece the Leaders are ahead in (or have won) ADDS its stake to the pot, and a piece the Trailers are ahead in (or have won) SUBTRACTS its stake. The pot is the resulting net amount.

Example: if the non-protected pieces tallied $20 for the Leaders and $2 for the Trailers, the pot would be the $18 difference. Those pieces no longer settle on their own — their value lives entirely in the pot.

Settling on the 18th Hole

The 18th hole decides the pot, based on the Leaders’ points on the hole (in a 2v2 the hole is the usual high-low, worth up to two points; in a 1v1 it is a single point). The protected games settle normally on top of this in every case:

  • Leaders win at least one point outright on 18 (net ahead on the hole) — the Leaders win TWICE the pot.
  • The 18th is a draw — this includes both balls halved, or a 1–1 split where each side wins one ball — the Leaders win the pot ONCE. Protected games pay out as they stood after 17 (a halve doesn’t change them).
  • Trailers win at least one point on 18 (net ahead on the hole) — the pot is VOID: nobody collects it. This is the “nothing” in double-or-nothing.

Tip: It is a true double-or-nothing for the Leaders: by winning the 18th they double the pot, by halving it they keep a single pot, and if the Trailers take the hole the whole pot disappears. Either way, the protected games still settle normally on top.

In the App

  • After the 17th hole, the Nassau match card shows the Big I offer for the trailing side. It lists the pot size and a comparison table of how the match settles for every 18th-hole result — “Not called” versus “Called 🌍” side by side.
  • In that table, amounts are shown from the trailing side’s point of view (+ means they win, − means they pay), and rows where calling beats not calling for them are highlighted — so the gamble is easy to read at a glance.
  • Tap “🌍 Call The International” to engage it (after both sides have agreed verbally). The card then shows a simple “🌍 International has been Called!” confirmation with an Undo button to reverse it before the round is saved.
  • Each individual game or press leg that is in the pot is marked with a 🌍 globe in the match card, placed at the cell where that piece settles: front-nine pieces (the Front 9 and a press’s front leg) show the globe at hole 9, and everything else (the Back 9, Total 18, back-nine presses, and a press’s back leg) shows it at hole 17. A press with both legs in the pot therefore shows two globes — one at hole 9 and one at hole 17. Protected games show their normal running totals with no globe.
  • Once the 18th is scored, the card shows the final Big I result and the overall match total.

7. The 6-Point Game

A three-player game where 6 points are distributed each hole based on net scores, with handicaps zeroed off the lowest-handicap player among the three.

Result on the hole Points awarded
One player has the outright lowest net score 6 to that player, 0 to the other two
Two players tie for lowest net 3 each to the tied players, 0 to the third
All three players tie 2 points each

Every hole distributes exactly 6 points. Across 18 holes the three players’ totals always add up to 108.

Tap the 6️⃣ icon at the bottom to see the 6-Point card. It uses the same Derrick style as the Nassau cards: Hole, HCP, and Par columns, then the three players’ gross scores (with red stroke circles based on the adjusted handicaps), a spacer column, and then the three players’ points columns, ending with a TOTAL PTS row.

Below that, the card shows the money standing per player. While the round is in progress this is a “CURRENT $” row — each player’s winnings based on the points so far, calculated the same way the final settlement is. Once all 18 holes are scored it becomes the final “$ WON/LOST” row. The 6-Point result is also included in the money summary on the master scorecard and in Round History.

8. Stableford

Stableford is a points game where you score points on each hole based on your net score relative to par — the better the hole, the more points. Unlike Nassau, a blow-up hole only costs you that hole’s points, never more, so it rewards steady play and keeps everyone in it to the last hole. Any two or more players in the round can be included.

Setup

  • In the Stableford card on the Setup screen (shown when there are 2 or more players), tap each player you want to include. Only the selected players are scored for Stableford.
  • Stableford uses each player’s deck-adjusted playing handicap to compute net scores, the same handicaps as the rest of the app.

Points Per Hole

Net score on the holePoints
Double bogey or worse0
Bogey (net)1
Par (net)2
Birdie (net)3
Eagle (net)4
Albatross (net)5

During & After the Round

  • Tap the Stableford tab in the bottom navigation to see the live points card — each player’s per-hole points and a running total, in the familiar Derrick scorecard style.
  • The player with the most points at the end wins. Standings update live as scores are entered.
  • Stableford results appear in Round History alongside the round’s other games, and are included in the round’s share/print output.

9. Match Cards

Match Cards lets you play a 1v1 Nassau against one or two golfers who are in other groups — players you aren’t scoring live on your own card. You keep your own scores during your round as usual; your opponents’ scores are entered later, in the clubhouse, once everyone is in. At the end of your round a separate ⚔️ Match Cards round is saved to History with your scores already filled in and the opponents’ columns blank, ready to score.

You play a separate 1v1 Nassau (Front 9 / Back 9 / Total 18 with automatic presses) against each opponent. Two opponents means two independent matches — your opponents do not play each other.

Setting Up Match Cards

  • Enable Match Cards on the Setup screen (it’s offered whenever there’s at least one player and Scorecard only is off). With a single player it’s the only game available.
  • Tap + Add Opponent and pick a golfer from your saved-players list. You can add up to two opponents. A player already in your live round can’t be picked, and the same person can’t be chosen for both opponent slots.
  • Each opponent shows their Index, a Tee deck selector, and the resulting Adjusted handicap. Pick their tee deck just like a regular player — the adjusted handicap recalculates automatically. They default to their saved favorite deck.
  • If an opponent has a Golf Canada ID on file, a green Golf Canada Fetch pill appears — tap it to pull their current handicap before the round.
  • Set the Nassau bet per side ($1, $2, $5, or $10) for each opponent.

Tip: All of the same Match Cards options are also available mid-round under ⚙️ Round, Match & Player Settings, so you can add or adjust opponents after you’ve teed off.

Scoring a Match Cards Round Later

When your round finishes, the ⚔️ Match Cards round lands in History with a purple border and a 📝 Score Round pill. It lists the participants (you and your opponents) so you can see who the match is against before scoring.

  • Tap Score Round to open it like a normal live scorecard — your scores are already filled in, your opponents’ columns are blank, and it starts on the first unscored hole. There are no “Update / Discard” buttons; you simply keep score.
  • Enter the opponents’ scores hole by hole. When the 18th hole is complete for everyone, the round auto-saves back to the same History entry (keeping its ⚔️ mark) — no duplicate is created — and you can start a New Round.
  • If you’re interrupted partway, use New Round → 💾 Save & Pause Round. The Match Cards round is re-saved with a ▶ Resume Scoring pill; tapping it jumps straight back to the first unentered hole.

Popups

11. Round History & Backup

Saving a Round

  • Once all 18 holes are scored, a 💾 Save Round button appears at the top of the Scorecard screen.
  • If you don’t save manually, the round is saved automatically when you confirm starting a New Round.

Viewing Past Rounds

Tap the 📜 History icon to see all saved rounds. Each entry shows the date, weather icon and temperature, pace of play, score pills for every player (your score is highlighted in dark green and gold), a colour-coded left border (green = played to handicap, gold = near handicap, red = tough day), and the games played. A ⚔️ Match Cards round shows a purple border and badge, and lists its participants. Tap any round to open a detailed view showing Final Totals, weather conditions, the full scorecard, Nassau match cards, 6-Point results, Stableford points, and the Snake settlement table.

Editing a History Round

Open any completed round from History and tap the ✏️ Edit button in the header. The round loads into the scorecard at hole 1 — all scores are fully editable, just like a live round. When done, tap 💾 Update History to save your changes back, or ↩ Discard Changes to revert. Weather, round date, and elapsed time are never modified. Note: you cannot edit a history round while an active round is in progress.

Paused Rounds — Save & Resume

If a round is interrupted (rain delay, early call-off), tap ⚙️ End Round Early → 💾 Save & Pause Round. The partial round is saved to History and you are returned to a clean Setup screen. Paused rounds show a ▶ Resume Round button in the History list. Tap it to reload the round at the first unscored hole and continue scoring normally — no games or settings are lost (including any Match Cards setup). When the round is complete and saved, it replaces the paused entry in History.

Tip: A paused ⚔️ Match Cards round shows a ▶ Resume Scoring pill instead, and resuming it jumps straight to the first unentered hole — see Section 9.

Backup & Restore

At the top of the History screen, a Backup & Restore card provides two buttons:

  • 📤 Export Backup — downloads a nassau-backup-YYYY-MM-DD.json file to your iPhone’s Files app. Contains all saved player contacts, round history, and the current round in progress.
  • 📥 Import Backup — reads a previously exported backup file and restores everything, showing a confirmation message when done.

Backup reminder: after every 10 rounds saved, the app prompts you to export a backup. Tap Go to History now? to go straight to the History screen and export. The reminder counts rounds since your last export — not total rounds.

⚠️ iOS can wipe a PWA’s local storage when you remove the Home Screen icon, run low on device storage, or in some iOS update scenarios. Export a backup regularly — especially before reinstalling or clearing Safari data. A backup after each round takes seconds.

Deleting a Round

From the History list, tap the ✕ button next to a round and confirm to permanently delete it.

13. The Snake 3-Putt Money Game

An optional money game that settles every 3 holes. Whoever held the snake at holes 3, 6, 9, 12, 15, and 18 pays each other participant the bet amount. The snake resets after each settlement — there are 6 independent stretches per round.

Setup

In the purple 🐍 Snake 3-Putt Game card on the Setup screen:

  • Check Enable Snake Game.
  • Choose the bet amount per player per settlement ($1, $2, $5, $10, or $20).
  • Select Participants — only checked players are in the game. Others are invisible to it.

Tracking During the Round

A compact bar appears above the player cards on each hole with a dropdown to record who 3-putted, and the current stretch holder shown on the right (e.g. “H4-6: Bruce”). After each settlement hole the stretch holder resets to — for the next stretch. Only participant names appear in the dropdown. You can go back to any past hole and change a 3-putt entry.

Settlement

At each settlement hole, whoever holds the snake pays each other participant the bet amount. Example: 5 players at $5 — the holder pays $20 ($5 × 4). If nobody held the snake in a stretch, that settlement is skipped.

The Snake Tab

Tap the 🐍 Snake tab in the bottom nav (visible when Snake is enabled) to see a Settlement Holes table (all 6 stretches colour-coded), Running Totals per player, and the current stretch holder during the round (hidden after hole 18).

Money Integration

Snake money is included in the live $ amounts beside each player on the scorecard, the 🐍 Snake $ row at the bottom of the master scorecard, and the Total $ Today total.

Each saved player can have a Score Wheel Default set in Manage Favorites & Players. Options are Par, Bogey, or Double — all relative to par on each hole.

15. Troubleshooting

My round disappeared

Use 💾 Save & Pause Round before ending a round early — it saves your partial round to History where you can resume it anytime. If you abandoned a round without saving, the data cannot be recovered.

The app looks outdated / not on the latest version

See “Keeping the App Current” in Section 1. Clear Safari site data for the app, force-close it, and reopen from the Home Screen icon.

Pop-ups aren’t appearing

Make sure you’re running from the Home Screen icon, not a Safari tab. Some dialogs only work correctly in standalone (installed) mode.

My player data or history disappeared

This can happen if you removed the Home Screen icon using “Remove App,” which wipes PWA local storage on iOS. If you have a backup file (exported from History), use 📥 Import Backup to restore it. To avoid this in future, always export a backup before removing or reinstalling the app, and never use Remove App — use the Safari reinstall method in Section 1 instead.

A player has quit, been injured, or left the round

If a player can no longer continue mid-round, tap ⚙️ Matches (or ⚙️ Players in a scorecard-only round) at the top of the Scorecard screen to open the manage screen. Remove them there; in a betting round this also drops them from any active Nassau and 6-Point games. Their scorecard column remains (with scores already entered) but they will no longer block the group from advancing holes or settling bets.

Tip: The missing-score warning banner is also tappable — tapping it opens Manage Matches directly, so you can remove the player without navigating away manually.

A score or game setting looks wrong

  • Handicaps can be edited mid-round by tapping the Idx value and pencil ✏️ on the player’s card.
  • Scores can be corrected at any time using the scroll wheel — all match totals, presses, and Birdie Juice credits recalculate automatically.
